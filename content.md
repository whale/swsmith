# Stephen W. Smith, PhD

Agricultural Engineer, Author, Photographer

---

## About

Stephen is an agricultural and water resources engineer with over 50 years in irrigation design, water conservation, and hands-on tree farming. A native of Roswell, New Mexico, his career has taken him across at least four continents. From the deserts of Kuwait, to — designing irrigation systems, teaching at Colorado State University, authoring the textbook on landscape irrigation, and running a farming enterprise in northern Colorado. He is a past president of the Irrigation Association, a Fellow of the American Society of Irrigation Consultants, and holds two patents for water supply research. When he's not working, he's fly fishing, woodworking, pestering his grown-ass kids with requests for updates, or photographing something wild along the Rocky Mountain range spanning from New Mexico to Montana and the least populated parts of everywhere between.

## From Stephen (rough draft)

> **Note:** Rough draft provided by Stephen — not final copy.

STEPHEN W. SMITH, PhD is an agricultural, irrigation, and water resources engineer. His engineering work provides consulting services in SCADA, canal modernization, water resources evaluation, and irrigation system design. His irrigation engineering experience includes projects throughout the United States, Europe, the Far East, and the Middle East.

Stephen founded Aqua Engineering, Inc. in 1975. For 25 years, Stephen has operated a farming enterprise, Buena Vida Farm, in Larimer County, CO. He was on the affiliate faculty at Colorado State University for 20 years. In 1996, he authored a textbook *Landscape Irrigation: Design and Management*. He is a founding partner of multiple companies and holds two patents associated with his water related research work. Stephen is past national president and a fellow of the American Society of Irrigation Consultants. In 2009, he was president of the Irrigation Association. For many years, Stephen served on the board and as vice president of Louden Irrigating Canal & Reservoir Co. Stephen is past head of the U.S. delegation to the International Standards Organization (ISO) TC 23 / SC 18 (irrigation and drainage) and is currently a water advisory committee member to the Foundation for Food and Agriculture research (FoundationFAR.com). With the 2018 formation of the Irrigation Innovation Consortium, Stephen agreed to serve as the interim Executive Director. In 2022, Stephen was elected to the Fort Collins Loveland Water District board of directors. In 2026, Stephen was nominated and selected to serve on the Corrales, NM Agricultural Land Conservation Commission.
