# ASIC Roy Williams Memorial Award — 2015

**Source:** https://asic.org/2015-stephen-smith/
**Published:** March 11, 2015

Dr. Stephen W. Smith, FASIC, CLIA, CAIS, was awarded the American Society of Irrigation Consultants Roy Williams Memorial Award — ASIC's highest achievement award — at the annual conference April 25-27, 2015 in Minneapolis, MN.

## Professional Summary (from award announcement)

- Owner of Wade Water LLC and Buena Vida Farm in Fort Collins, CO
- Specialties: irrigation and water rights engineering, irrigation automation and control (SCADA), canal modernization
- 40 years of experience in irrigation engineering and water resources
- Founded Aqua Engineering, Inc. in 1975 — originally created to provide highly specialized irrigation engineering services to municipal and state agencies, universities, and private developers
- Prior to 1975: Colorado State University research faculty — irrigation research and evaluation of irrigation technologies
- Native of New Mexico
- Irrigation projects throughout the U.S. and numerous countries in Europe, the Far East, and the Middle East
- Taught irrigation design at Colorado State University for almost 20 years
- U.S. Head of Delegation to two International Standards Organization (ISO) committees involving irrigation and water reuse
- Published textbook: Landscape Irrigation: Design and Management (1996), plus technical papers
- PhD dissertation: patent-pending approach to meeting municipal demands for safe yield water supplies without drying up farms
- Past national president of ASIC and Society Fellow
- Irrigation Association board of directors; president in 2009
- Three degrees in Agricultural Engineering from Colorado State University and New Mexico State University

## In His Own Words

"We are such a close and dynamic industry. Irrigation educators and practitioners are hungry for innovative ideas and technology that can be brought to bear affordably. That's what drew me to ASIC in the 1980s – steadfast commitment to exploring and sharing irrigation innovation. The Society has always directed an unblinking focus on water conservation and unbiased, third-party support to clientele. The Roy Williams Award is quite gratifying and absolutely a milestone in my professional career."

## About the Award

Named after an ASIC founder and one of the original independent irrigation consultants. Given for significant contributions to the irrigation industry. Not an annual accolade — given as the Society deems appropriate. Formerly the ASIC Industry Achievement Award, renamed after Roy Williams in 1988.
